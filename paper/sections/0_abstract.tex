\begin{abstract}
We consolidate, in a single manuscript, five empirical and computational tests of the Discrete Symplectic Cosmology (DSC) framework: a non-autonomous, weakly damped lattice cosmology in which a $1/\ln^2(t)$ adiabatic cooling law---inspired by the spectral statistics of the Riemann zeta zeros---is the central ansatz tying microscopic dynamics to time-dependent cosmological parameters.
The framework predicts, simultaneously, a smooth Hubble relaxation $H(t) = H_\infty + \beta/\ln^2(t/t_P)$, a logarithmic dark-energy term $\Lambda(t) = \Lambda_\infty + \gamma/\ln^2(t/t_P)$, and a slow drift in the fine-structure constant $\alpha$.
Our headline findings are:
(i)~Fitting the four anchor $H_0$ measurements (SH0ES local distance ladder, JWST replication, Planck CMB, BAO) by $H(t) = H_\infty + \beta/\ln^2(t/t_P)$ yields $R^2 = 0.91$, $\chi^2/{\rm dof} = 0.28$, recasting the SH0ES--Planck Hubble tension as a smooth relaxation rather than a discrepancy.
(ii)~A CAMB + DESI BAO Fisher analysis gives $\sigma(\gamma_{\rm eff}) \sim 1.2 \times 10^4$ for the dark-energy correction, so DSC is currently \emph{indistinguishable from} $\Lambda$CDM at CMB+BAO precision; the framework is not currently in tension with these data, but neither does it improve the fit.
(iii)~Five late-time stability criteria (background expansion, growth, ghost / gradient / bounce conditions) are satisfied for $\gamma_{\rm eff} \in [0, 10]$, so the framework is internally consistent at the linear level.
(iv)~On the published King~\emph{et al.}~(2012) 295-absorber quasar catalog, the DSC drift is statistically indistinguishable from a constant null ($\Delta {\rm BIC} = +5.7$ favors null), with the joint atomic-clock + quasar bound $|\Gamma| < 0.30$; on a 128-absorber subset previously analyzed in the literature, the same model gives $\Delta\chi^2 = 31.5$ improvement over null but with negative $R^2$, a tension between subsets that we report rather than gloss.
(v)~A weakly damped St\"ormer--Verlet lattice surrogate of CMB anisotropies, evaluated against the CAMB Planck-2018 best-fit $D_\ell^{TT}$, produces a poor quantitative fit ($\chi^2/{\rm dof} \approx 80$ over $\ell \in [30, 256]$) and lattice $D(k)$-peaks that map to $\ell \approx 30, 60, 90$ rather than to the observed $\ell \approx 220, 540, 810$; we retract any quantitative claim of reproducing the Planck $C_\ell$.
A differentiable JAX implementation of the lattice surrogate enables gradient-based reconstruction with $r = 0.98$ in-sample pixel correlation, but a held-out hemisphere test ($r = -0.17$) confirms that the inverse problem is severely underdetermined.
We present the lattice surrogate as a methodological building block, position the Hubble-anchor and stability results as the framework's strongest empirical support to date, and explicitly flag the King-2012 quasar tension and the low CMB-spectrum sensitivity as the directions that need further data to falsify or sustain the framework.
\end{abstract}
