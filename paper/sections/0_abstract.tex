\begin{abstract}
We consolidate, in a single manuscript, eleven empirical and computational tests of the Discrete Symplectic Cosmology (DSC) framework: a non-autonomous, weakly damped lattice cosmology in which a $1/\ln^2(t)$ adiabatic cooling law---inspired by the spectral statistics of the Riemann zeta zeros---is the central ansatz tying microscopic dynamics to time-dependent cosmological parameters.
The framework predicts, simultaneously, a smooth Hubble relaxation $H(t) = H_\infty + \beta/\ln^2(t/t_P)$, a logarithmic dark-energy term $\Lambda(t) = \Lambda_\infty + \gamma/\ln^2(t/t_P)$, and a slow drift in the fine-structure constant $\alpha$.

At the level of widely-separated anchor points the framework looks promising: a four-anchor $H_0$ fit (SH0ES, JWST, Planck, BAO) by $H(t) = H_\infty + \beta/\ln^2(t/t_P)$ achieves $R^2 = 0.91$, $\chi^2/{\rm dof} = 0.28$ (Pillar~C); five late-time stability criteria are satisfied for $\gamma_{\rm eff} \in [0, 10]$ (Pillar~E); and on the 128-absorber Murphy~2003 Keck/HIRES quasar catalog, the DSC drift improves the fit by $\Delta\chi^2 = 31.5$ ($5.6\sigma$) under per-absorber fit-error-only weighting, reducing to $\Delta\chi^2 = 7.8$ ($2.8\sigma$) under Murphy's published $\sigma_{\rm rand}$-augmented budget (Pillar~A; we report both).

However, dense high-precision data tell a different story.
On the standard 32-point cosmic chronometer compilation (Pillar~F), the DSC ansatz reduces to a constant $H_0$ and gives $\chi^2/{\rm dof} = 4.0$ versus $\Lambda$CDM's $0.49$; on Pantheon$+$ (1580 SNe; Pillar~G) DSC is rejected at $\Delta\chi^2 = +617$; on DESI~DR2 BAO (Pillar~H) DSC gives $\chi^2 \approx 14000$.
On the joint DESI$+$Pantheon$+$+CC dataset (1623 constraints; Pillar~J), DSC is rejected at $\Delta\chi^2 = +37000$ ($\sim 190\sigma$-equivalent), while CPL ($w_0w_a$) is mildly preferred over $\Lambda$CDM at $\Delta {\rm BIC} = -2.6$.
A cut-by-cut subset analysis of the King~2012 295-absorber catalog (Pillar~K) shows that Pillar~A's apparent signal is essentially the long-standing Keck $\alpha$-dipole signal: VLT-only and Keck-only subsets give $\Gamma$ of opposite sign, consistent with prior reports of wavelength-calibration systematics, while none of 200 random 128-subsamples reach Pillar~A's reported $\Delta\chi^2$.
Finally, a weakly damped St\"ormer--Verlet lattice surrogate of CMB anisotropies, evaluated against the CAMB Planck-2018 best-fit $D_\ell^{TT}$, yields $\chi^2/{\rm dof} \approx 80$ over $\ell \in [30, 256]$, and the lattice $D(k)$-peaks map under the geometric projection to $\ell \approx 30, 60, 90$ rather than to $\ell \approx 220, 540, 810$.

The simple DSC ansatz is therefore strongly disfavored as a competitive model of the late universe.
What survives is more limited: (i) a logarithmic relaxation between recombination and the present provides a phenomenologically convenient description of the four-anchor Hubble tension, (ii) the framework is internally consistent at the linear level, and (iii) the differentiable JAX lattice surrogate is a useful methodological building block for simulation-based inference workflows.
We retract any quantitative claim of reproducing the Planck $C_\ell$, the late-time $H(z)$ shape, the BAO distance ladder, or the cosmic-web structure formation; the simple $1/\ln^2$ ansatz must be modified or extended for any of those targets.
The negative results F--K are themselves valuable as falsifiers and may guide future work on the same functional family.
\end{abstract}
