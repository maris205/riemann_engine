\begin{abstract}
We explore whether the Discrete Symplectic Cosmology (DSC) framework---in which cosmic evolution is modeled as a non-autonomous, weakly damped symplectic map on a Planck-scale lattice with a $1/\ln^2(t)$ adiabatic cooling law inspired by the spectral statistics of the Riemann zeta zeros---can reproduce key statistical features of the cosmic microwave background (CMB) temperature anisotropies.
This study is exploratory: the lattice serves as an effective surrogate model with no claim of being a Planck-scale theory, and the connection to the Riemann zeros is taken as a working motivation rather than an established physical correspondence.
Using a St\"ormer--Verlet integrator on two- and three-dimensional lattices, we find that:
(i) for the parameter range we explore the symplectic evolution is approximately linear, so near-Gaussian one-point statistics (skewness $= +0.07 \pm 0.30$, excess kurtosis $= -0.28 \pm 0.24$ over 20 realizations) are consistent with expectations rather than a non-trivial emergence; the contrast with a dissipative coupled-map-lattice baseline (kurtosis $= -1.60$) reflects multiple combined differences in the dynamics rather than a clean isolation of the symplectic property;
(ii) acoustic-like oscillation peaks emerge in the lattice power spectrum $D(k) = k^2 P(k)$, but these peaks are characteristic of the lattice geometry: under the geometric projection $\ell \approx 2\pi R_{\rm shell}\,k$ they map to $\ell \approx 30, 60, \ldots$ rather than to the observed $\ell \approx 220, 540, 810$;
(iii) the $1/\ln^2(t)$ cooling produces a threshold-defined effective freeze-out scale, but the formal sound-horizon integral diverges logarithmically and the reported value is sensitive to threshold and grid parameters, so we no longer claim a physical analog of the baryon--photon sound horizon;
(iv) full-sky maps from a $128^3$ lattice projected onto a HEALPix sphere have pixel distributions close to those of Planck SMICA, but quantitative comparison to the CAMB Planck-2018 angular power spectrum yields $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} \approx 80$ over $\ell \in [30, 256]$ with a 20\% per-$\ell$ error budget, far above acceptable agreement; the model does \emph{not} reproduce the Planck $C_\ell$ at the level of individual multipoles.
A differentiable JAX implementation enables gradient-based reconstruction of a 3D primordial field with high in-sample pixel correlation ($r = 0.98$), but a held-out hemisphere test gives $r = -0.17$, confirming that the inverse problem is severely underdetermined ($\sim 10^6$ unknowns vs.\ $\sim 10^4$ observed pixels) and that the recovered 3D field cannot be interpreted as a physical primordial reconstruction.
We position the DSC lattice as a methodological proof-of-concept for differentiable lattice surrogates of CMB-like fluctuations, and explicitly retract any quantitative claim of reproducing the observed CMB acoustic peak structure.
\end{abstract}
